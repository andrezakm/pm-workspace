# Interview: Marcus Thorne (VP of Sales)
Date: 2025-10-21
Topic: Market Penetration

**Interviewer:** Marcus, thanks for seeing me. I like the blazer! Is that from that place on 5th?

**Marcus:** Good eye. Yeah, I had a meeting with the board this morning, had to look the part. You know how it is—dress for the job you want, or the job you're trying to save, right? (Laughs)

**Interviewer:** (Laughs) Fair point. So, speaking of the job—are we hitting our territory coverage goals? How’s the map looking?

**Marcus:** Honestly? Not even close. We’re scratching the surface. It's frustrating. The market is huge, there's a literal ocean of opportunity out there, but we’re moving like we're neck-deep in molasses. We’re too sniper-like. I mean, I love a good marksman, but right now I need shotgun coverage with sniper precision. I need both.

**Interviewer:** That’s a tough balance to strike. What’s the main thing slowing the team down? Is it the training?

**Marcus:** Paralysis by analysis. 100%. My team overthinks every single move. They want every duck in a row, every data point verified, every star aligned before they even think about dialing a number. They tell me "research takes time, Marcus." I say, "Go faster. The competition isn't waiting for your ducks to line up."

**Interviewer:** What does that research actually entail for them? Is it just deep-diving into prospects?

**Marcus:** Who knows? It’s a black box. I walk past the desks and they’re just... on the internet. They’re "gathering intel." Look, I get it. You need to know who you’re talking to. You need to know if they are hiring, if they just raised money, if they're about to go under. But does it really take 15 minutes per prospect? 15 minutes! If you do the math, that's only 4 prospects an hour. That's a joke. 

**Interviewer:** Where do you think they're primarily getting this data? 

**Marcus:** It’s all out there in plain sight. LinkedIn, Press Releases, Google News. It’s not secret, it's not tucked away in some dark corner of the web. It’s just unstructured. They’re turning unstructured noise into structured talk tracks manually. That’s the bottleneck. It's the "manual" part that kills me. If we could automate that "intel gathering" piece—just hand them the dossier—we’d be unstoppable. But for now, I just have to keep yelling at them to make more calls and hope they pick up the pace.
