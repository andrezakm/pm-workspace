# Interview: Mike Ross (Sales Director)
Date: 2025-10-14
Topic: Lead Generation Process

**Interviewer:** Mike, good to see you again. How's the view from the corner office?

**Mike:** Ha, "corner office." You mean the glass fishbowl? It's fine. A bit noisy today. The construction next door is driving me nuts. *Drilling sound in background*. You hear that?

**Interviewer:** Oof, yeah, that is loud. I'll try to speak up. So, diving in—what’s the biggest drag on your team’s performance right now? What keeps you up at night?

**Mike:** It’s the top of the funnel. Always is. My guys are great closers, absolute sharks, but they’re starving, man. They are starving for good meetings. We have this "lead gen" team, right? A dozen bright kids. But honestly, I don't know what they do all day. They say they’re researching, but the output is a trickle. Droplets. 

**Interviewer:** Droplets, wow. Why do you think that is? Are they just slow?

**Mike:** They tell me "Mike, it’s hard to find the right people." I don't buy it. Everyone is online! My grandma is online! But then I sit with them, and yeah... it looks tedious. They’re jumping between five different tabs. Corporate website, some news aggregator, LinkedIn, maybe Crunchbase. They say they need all these "signals" to qualify a lead before they reach out. It's like they're writing a biography before they say hello.

**Interviewer:** Do you think all those signals are necessary? Or is it overkill?

**Mike:** I don't know. Maybe? They claim that if they don't check a prospect's recent activity on LinkedIn, they’ll look stupid on the call. So they spend 20 minutes reading the guy’s posts about his dog or his vacation to Cabo just to write one email. It’s inefficient. But they insist it's the only way to get a response. I just want them on the phones. Dialing! 

**Interviewer:** Is there a specific source that yields the best results? Or are they just guessing?

**Mike:** Hard to say. They attribute leads to "Outbound" in the CRM, but that could mean anything. I suspect it's mostly just them stalking people on social media until they find an opening. It feels very manual. We need to automate this, we need to speed it up. But I don't want to lose that "personal touch" they keep talking about. That's the Catch-22. I want speed, they want quality, and right now we have neither.
