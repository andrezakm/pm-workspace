# Interview: Sarah Jenkins (Sales Development Rep)
Date: 2025-10-12
Topic: Lead Generation Bottlenecks

**Interviewer:** Hey Sarah! Thanks so much for taking the time to chat. I know it's near the end of the quarter, so you must be slammed. How’s your week holding up?

**Sarah:** Ugh, honestly? Is it Friday yet? I feel like I’ve been running on caffeine and adrenaline since Monday. It’s a total mess. The coffee machine on the 4th floor is broken again, which is basically a crisis in itself. But yeah, just trying to hit numbers. You know how it is.

**Interviewer:** Oh no, the good coffee machine? That is a crisis. Aside from the caffeine shortage, where are you actually spending most of your time today? Walk me through the daily grind.

**Sarah:** Okay, so... I spend half my day just trying to figure out who I’m supposed to actually call. Like, I get this list from marketing, right? And—no offense to marketing, bless their hearts—but it’s absolute garbage. 

**Interviewer:** Garbage how? Like, bad names?

**Sarah:** Worse. It's zombie data. Half the emails bounce immediately, or I call and they’re like "Who?" or the person left the company three years ago to go raise alpacas in Vermont. So before I can even think about pitching, I have to go double-check everything manually. It’s exhausting.

**Interviewer:** That sounds painful. Can you walk me through that "double-checking" process? What specific tabs do you have open?

**Sarah:** Okay, picture this: I have the CRM on one screen—Salesforce, dragging as usual. Then I pop open LinkedIn in a new tab to see if the person is actually still employed there. If they aren’t, which is like 40% of the time, I have to go hunt for who the new person is. And it’s not just LinkedIn. I’m looking at their company website, checking Google News for recent PR, sometimes I’m even on, like, obscure industry forums or Reddit just to see if they’re active. It feels like I’m a private investigator or a stalker, not a salesperson. 

**Interviewer:** Hah, "Detective Jenkins." It sounds incredibly time-consuming though.

**Sarah:** It’s brutal. Seriously. I’m supposed to be making 50 calls a day. That's the KPI. But I’m lucky if I get to 20 because I’m stuck copy-pasting job titles from LinkedIn profiles into Salesforce fields. And then half the time the formatting is weird—like all caps or weird spacing—so I have to fix that too. I swear, if I didn't have to verify every single lead, I could double my quota. Easy. It’s just... so much noise.

**Interviewer:** Do you use any tools to help with this? Or are you just winging it?

**Sarah:** We have ZoomInfo, which is okay, but it’s super expensive so we only have a few licenses to share. I end up just Googling stuff half the time because the ZoomInfo login is always taken. Or I just doom-scroll through my own LinkedIn feed hoping to spot someone changing jobs or announcing a promotion. It’s totally random. It feels like I'm fishing without bait.
