# Interview: Jessica Chen (Account Executive)
Date: 2025-10-15
Topic: Prospecting Habits

**Interviewer:** Jessica, thanks for squeezing me in. I saw the leaderboard in the hallway—you are absolutely crushing it this quarter. Top slot again! What’s your secret?

**Jessica:** (Laughs) Oh, you saw that? I try not to brag, but... yeah. My secret? I don't sleep. No, seriously, I sleep like four hours a night. I'm kidding. Mostly. Ideally, I’d just take meetings that the SDRs set up. That's the dream, right? But the quality... it just isn't there.

**Interviewer:** Really? So you're doing double duty?

**Jessica:** Pretty much. If I relied on inbound, I'd miss quota. So I do my own prospecting at night after my kid goes to sleep. It’s my "second shift."

**Interviewer:** Wow. Walking the extra mile. What does that nightly routine look like?

**Jessica:** Okay, so I pour a glass of wine (don't tell HR), and I have a system. I look for companies that just raised a Series B. That's the sweet spot. Then I go to LinkedIn Sales Navigator—which I pay for myself, by the way, because the company is too cheap to give it to AEs—and I map out the decision makers. I’m looking for the VP of Ops or the CTO. I check their tenure. If they’ve been there less than 6 months, they’re perfect. Fresh blood. They want to buy new stuff to prove themselves. 

**Interviewer:** That sounds unbelievably structured. Impressive.

**Jessica:** It is, but it takes forever. Finding the funding news is the easy part. But then matching that to the right person on LinkedIn, finding their email... it's a nightmare. I use like three different chrome extensions just to guess their email address. It’s a total grind. I spend maybe 4 hours a night just building a list of 10 people to email the next day. 4 hours! 

**Interviewer:** 4 hours for 10 people? That seems... high.

**Jessica:** I know, right? But it works. Because I have to know *everything* about them. I read their profiles, check who they follow, read their articles. If I could just snap my fingers and have that list of "New CTOs at Series B companies" with their emails, I’d kill it. I'd double my numbers. But instead, I’m manually copying data into a spreadsheet at midnight. It feels like simple data entry, not strategic sales. I'm overqualified for this part.
