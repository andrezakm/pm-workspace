# Interview: Rachel Green (Junior SDR)
Date: 2025-10-20
Topic: Onboarding and early struggles

**Interviewer:** Hey Rachel! Good to see you. How’s the first couple of months been? I remember when I started, I couldn't even find the breakroom for a week.

**Rachel:** (Laughs) Oh, same! I literally walked into a closet on my second day thinking it was the kitchen. It’s been... a lot. There’s so much to learn. The product is complex—I'm still figuring out the difference between the API and the SDK—but honestly, the hardest part isn't the technical stuff. It's just building the list.

**Interviewer:** Building the list? I thought marketing just handed you guys a big spreadsheet of gold?

**Rachel:** I wish! I do get leads, but my manager says I can't just "blast" them. I need to "enrich" them before I reach out. I need to find a "hook." Something personal. So I’m basically stalking these strangers online. I found one guy’s Twitter where he talked about sourdough bread—like, he has a whole thread on starters—so I mentioned that in an email. He actually replied! But that took me 30 minutes to find. 30 minutes for one email!

**Interviewer:** That’s a lot of sourdough research. Is that sustainable for 50 leads?

**Rachel:** I don't know. Everyone else seems to do it faster. I feel like I’m looking in the wrong places. I check their company blog, their annual reports... sometimes I can't find anything personal. Then I just go to LinkedIn and see they liked a post about "Digital Transformation"—which everyone likes—and I use that. It feels a bit fake, honestly. A bit forced.

**Interviewer:** Does everyone rely on LinkedIn that much? Is there no other way?

**Rachel:** Yeah. It’s basically the bible here. If it’s not on LinkedIn, it didn't happen. I wish I could just pull that info automatically. Like, "Show me everyone who posted about Cloud Migration in the last week." That would save me hours of scrolling. Instead, I’m manually scrolling feeds, squinting at photos, trying to see what books are on their shelf. It's exhausting. I just want to talk to people, not be a professional lurker.
