# Interview: Emily Blunt (Marketing Manager)
Date: 2025-10-18
Topic: Lead Quality and Sales Alignment

**Interviewer:** Emily, thanks for meeting me. Love the office decor. Is that a real fiddle-leaf fig?

**Emily:** Thanks! And no, it’s fake. I killed three real ones, so I gave up. I can keep leads alive, but plants? No chance.

**Interviewer:** Hah, better leads than plants, I guess. Speaking of leads—there seems to be some... let's call it "tension" between Marketing and Sales regarding lead quality. Can you spill the tea?

**Emily:** "Tension"? That's a diplomatic word for it. It’s the classic story, right? A tale as old as time. We generate hundreds of MQLs, beautiful leads, and Sales says they’re trash. Absolute garbage. But they aren't even working them! We get leads from webinars, whitepaper downloads, expensive events. 

**Interviewer:** And what does Sales do with them?

**Emily:** They cherry-pick. That's what they do. They ignore 90% of what we send—literally leave them rotting in the queue—and just go hunt on LinkedIn for their "ideal accounts". It drives me crazy. We spend serious budget—my budget—getting these people into the funnel, and the SDRs are like "Oh, this person isn't a Director level, I'm not calling."

**Interviewer:** Why do you think they prefer their own research over your leads?

**Emily:** Because they're perfectionists. Or maybe just picky. They want the perfect lead. The "Glengarry" lead. They want someone who is ready to buy *now*. They spend hours snooping on prospects online to find that "golden ticket". They say our leads lack context. "Oh, we don't know their tech stack, Emily." Well, ask them! Get on the phone and ask them! But no, they want to find it on a job description or a LinkedIn profile first. They want the layup.

**Interviewer:** Is that information they want usually available publicly?

**Emily:** Usually, yes. But it’s scattered. You have to look at the company page, maybe check what their engineers are posting about. It’s a lot of manual correlation. I get why they do it—conversion is higher if you know the context, I admit that—but it doesn't scale. They can't do that for 100 leads a day. So they do it for 5 and ignore the rest. And we lose pipeline. It's heartbreaking, really.
