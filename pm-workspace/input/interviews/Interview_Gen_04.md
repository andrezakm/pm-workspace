# Interview: Tom Baker (Senior Sales Rep)
Date: 2025-10-16
Topic: The "Modern" Sales Process

**Interviewer:** Tom, glad to have you. You've been in this game longer than anyone else on the floor. How are you finding things these days?

**Tom:** Game? Hah, it used to be a game. Now it's a... I don't know what it is. A science project? But I'm good. Knees are acting up a bit with this weather, but I'm here. 

**Interviewer:** Sorry to hear about the knees. So, since you've seen it all—how has the job changed since you started?

**Tom:** It’s all computers now. It's all screens. Back in the day, you bought a list, you picked up the phone, and you dialed. You had a conversation. Now? It’s "social selling" this, "multi-channel sequencing" that. It’s a lot of noise. Just buzzwords.

**Interviewer:** Do you think it’s effective though? This new way?

**Tom:** Look, people are people. They haven't changed. They buy from people they like. That hasn't changed in 50 years. But I have to admit, it’s harder to get them on the phone now. Nobody answers unknown numbers. So yeah, I have to play the game. My manager wants me on LinkedIn posting "thought leadership". I hate it. I feel like a teenager trying to be an influencer.

**Interviewer:** "Influencer Tom," I like it. So if the phone is dead, how do you find your leads then?

**Tom:** I try to stick to what I know. Referrals. Handshakes. But I do look them up. You kinda have to. I had a meeting last week where the guy asked me if I saw his article on AI ethics. I hadn’t. It was embarrassing. I looked like a dinosaur. So now I have to check their LinkedIn profile before every call. It’s just another box to check, another hoop to jump through.

**Interviewer:** Does that take a lot of time? That research?

**Tom:** Too much. Way too much. I’m clicking around looking for "triggers" or whatever they call them. I’d rather be golfing with them. But you can’t get the meeting without the "research" now. It feels like half the job is just staring at a screen trying to find an angle. And the data in our CRM? It's always wrong anyway. "Oh, he's at Oracle." No, he left six months ago, look at his LinkedIn! It’s annoying. I want to sell, not be a data clerk.
