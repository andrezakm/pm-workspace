# Interview: Sam Petit (Enterprise Account Executive)
Date: 2025-10-22
Topic: Enterprise Account Planning

**Interviewer:** Sam, thanks for jumping on. I know your calendar is a nightmare. Is that a whiteboard behind you? It looks like a mad scientist’s lab.

**Sam:** (Laughs) It kind of is! My wife calls it my "conspiracy wall." I'm just trying to make sense of this new Fortune 500 account. It's a beast.

**Interviewer:** I bet. Enterprise sales is a different animal entirely. How do you even begin to approach a new account of that scale?

**Sam:** It’s an investigation, plain and simple. I’m not just looking for one person or one "buyer." I need to map the whole ecosystem. Who reports to who? Who’s the influencer who has the CEO's ear? Who’s the blocker who's going to kill the deal because they like the incumbent? It’s complex.

**Interviewer:** And how do you actually map that out? Do they just hand you an org chart?

**Sam:** In my dreams! No, it’s a puzzle with missing pieces. We never have the real org chart. So I go to LinkedIn. I live on LinkedIn. I look at the "People also viewed" sidebar on the profiles—that’s a goldmine. I look at common connections. I try to piece together the hierarchy based on endorsements and group memberships. "Okay, this VP was endorsed for 'Strategy' by this Director, they probably work closely..." 

**Interviewer:** That sounds incredibly manual. Like you're some kind of digital detective.

**Sam:** It is! I literally draw it out on this whiteboard. "John knows Sarah, Sarah used to work with Mike at Oracle, so they have a history." It’s all there on the screen, scattered across fifty tabs, but connecting the dots takes real brain power. And it takes serious time. I spend days—literal days—just mapping an account before I even send the first "hello" email. 

**Interviewer:** Days? Wow. Is there a lot of noise you have to filter out?

**Sam:** Tons of it. People thank "everyone" in their posts, titles are completely made up—"Head of Global Synergy" could mean anything. You have to read between the lines, check their history, see what they actually talk about. It’s 80% reading LinkedIn profiles and 20% actual sales strategy. Which, when you think about it, is completely backwards. I should be strategizing, I should be building relationships, not doing low-level detective work. It's a waste of my experience, but I have to do it to win.
